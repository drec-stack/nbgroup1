Industrial design focuses primarily on the physical appearance, ergonomics, and user interaction of mass-produced products. It deals with form, materials, manufacturing processes, and how users physically interact with products.

Product design is a broader discipline that encompasses the entire product development process. It includes industrial design but also extends to research, strategy, user experience, engineering, and business considerations. Product designers think about the complete product ecosystem, including digital interfaces, services, and business models.

At NB Group, we practice integrated product design that combines both disciplines for holistic solutions.

Design thinking is a human-centered problem-solving methodology that emphasizes empathy, collaboration, and experimentation. It's an iterative process that helps teams understand users, challenge assumptions, redefine problems, and create innovative solutions.

1. Empathize

Understand user needs, emotions, and experiences through observation and engagement

2. Define

Articulate the core problem based on user insights and research findings

3. Ideate

Generate diverse creative solutions through brainstorming and exploration

4. Prototype

Create tangible representations of ideas for testing and feedback

5. Test

Validate solutions with real users and iterate based on feedback

Intellectual property rights are clearly defined in our project agreements. Typically, once the design project is completed and all payments are made, full ownership of the designs is transferred to the client. This includes all design files, documentation, and associated intellectual property.

Key aspects of our IP policy:

  • Client Ownership: You retain full rights to use, modify, and commercialize the designs
  • Clear Documentation: All IP transfer details are specified in our project contracts
  • Portfolio Rights: We typically request permission to showcase completed work in our portfolio
  • Background IP: Any pre-existing IP brought to the project remains with its original owner

We recommend consulting with legal counsel for specific IP concerns, and we're happy to work with your legal team to ensure all agreements meet your requirements.
